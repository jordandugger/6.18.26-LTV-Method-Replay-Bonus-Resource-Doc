Section 04
Constraint Diagnosis
P1 — CRITICAL No Named Mechanism or Packaged Offer
Joyce has no offer in the IFCA sense of the word. There is no program name, no defined duration, no price, no deliverable, and no mechanism name. “Strength training with muscle awareness and some massage” is a description of what she does, not a reason to buy. Prospects cannot buy something they cannot conceptualize. The LMT credential is irrelevant until it is wrapped in a named, priced program with a clear outcome promise.
Fix — This Week
Name the mechanism: “The Train. Reset. Rebuild. Method” or “The Strength + Recovery Protocol.” Build the offer: 12-week 1:1 program, 2x/week training + 1x massage therapy session/month, $1,500 upfront. That is the pitch. Write it down, put it on the primalrosefit page, and put the link in the Instagram bio. No website build required — update the existing page.
P1 — CRITICAL Zero Digital Discoverability — Invisible to Cold Prospects
Instagram requires login to view. Facebook requires login to view. No ads running. Zero DM funnel. No opt-in. The only acquisition channel is word-of-mouth from one gym owner. At current trajectory, the number of clients Joyce has will not grow unless that gym owner refers more people. That is a single-point-of-failure business with no leverage. Joyce is not in competition with other online coaches — she is simply not findable.
Fix — Day 1
Make Instagram public immediately. This is a 30-second settings change. Then post the credential reveal reel (Hook 01) with “Comment RESET” CTA. Install ManyChat free tier, connect the keyword trigger, and set up a 3-message DM flow that ends with a booking link. Total setup time: 2–3 hours. Zero ad spend required.
P2 — GAP Confidence / Self-Promotion Mindset Block
Joyce self-reported her primary bottleneck as “getting more confident in myself and how to promote myself.” This is the honest answer, and it matters — but it is also the most expensive bottleneck of all, because every week of inaction has a real dollar cost. The reframe: promotion is not self-aggrandizement, it is simply telling people that a service that could genuinely help them exists. Joyce’s LMT credential, used correctly, helps people who are stuck in pain loops and injury cycles. Not promoting it means those people do not get help.
Framework Shift
The camera is not about Joyce — it is about the client watching. Every piece of content Joyce makes should start from this question: “Who is in pain right now that I can help, and what do they need to hear?” The credential reveal reel is not “look at me” content — it is “I have something that can help you” content. That reframe makes the first post easier.
P3 — WATCH Single Acquisition Source (Gym Owner Dependency)
The current referral-only model works at 2 clients but will not scale to 4 without either more referral partners or an owned digital channel. This becomes urgent at the Growth phase, but should be addressed now by building the Instagram funnel so Joyce is not entirely dependent on one relationship.
Monitor
This resolves naturally once Instagram is public and posting consistently. Add a second referral partner (local chiropractor, physical therapist, or sports medicine clinic in Boise) as a parallel track. Joyce’s LMT license makes her a natural referral partner for clinical practices — this is a relationship worth pursuing in months 2–3.
OK — VALIDATED Offer Proof + Working Acquisition Seed
Two paying clients from a cold referral source means the offer converts when presented by a trusted authority. The gym owner is not doing sales work — the referral alone is enough for Joyce to close. That close rate (100% from warm referral) is the baseline for what happens when prospects arrive pre-sold. The work is to replicate the trust environment digitally: social proof content, mechanism name, and authority positioning do the same job the gym owner is currently doing in person.
