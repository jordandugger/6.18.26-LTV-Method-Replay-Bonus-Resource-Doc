Constraint Diagnosis
P1 · Critical Constraint — Jordan Confirmed Live
Catastrophic Under-Pricing Destroys All Downstream Economics
$1,200 for 4 months = $300/month. At this price, Cindy cannot run profitable ads (CAC would consume 100% of the offer value), cannot afford to hire support, cannot demonstrate enough commitment from clients to ensure they get results, and actively signals to premium buyers that she is a budget option. The pricing alone is doing more damage than any marketing problem. Jordan explicitly called this out in the live hot seat: "raise the price immediately."
The Fix (Jordan's exact recommendation): Immediately raise to $3,500 for 4 months, or $6,000 for a 12-month program. Present both in the sales call — 4 months with an upgrade path, or 12 months for committed clients. Do NOT grandfather existing clients. New price applies to all new calls starting now. This single action will simultaneously improve call quality, close rate (premium pricing = more committed buyers), and margin for ad spend.
P1 · Critical Constraint
Audience-Offer Mismatch — Wrong Buyers Attracted by Current Creative
Cindy's 66K audience was built primarily through her athletic competition content and general faith-fitness posts. This has attracted a following that skews toward young athletes and fitness enthusiasts who admire her Olympic achievement but are not the $6,000 coaching buyer. Her calls are full of people who "can't afford it" — but that is a symptom of attracting the wrong person, not of pricing too high. The offer needs to repel non-buyers actively.
The Fix: Build a parallel content track of "pricing filter" content — explicitly target successful Christian women with careers and disposable income, explicitly mention the investment level in content, and use ex-athlete identity as the avatar qualifier. "If you were a competitive athlete who wants to feel and look like one again, I work with you." Most of the current 66K will self-select out — that is the goal. The 500 who self-select in are worth more than the 66,000 who admire from a distance.
P2 · Meaningful Gap
Olympian Credential Leading as Background — Not as Hook
Three Olympic appearances is the single most powerful credibility signal available in the fitness coaching space. Most coaches would pay six figures for this authority if they could. Cindy has it and is leading with "metabolism reset tips." This is the equivalent of having a PhD from MIT and opening your business card with "I like to read." The credential needs to be in the first sentence of every ad, every bio, every DM opener, every sales call intro.
The Fix: Rebuild every hook to lead with the Olympian identity. Every talking-head ad starts with: "I competed at 3 Olympics, and what I learned about how women's bodies actually respond to training..." Every bio says "3× Olympian coaching faith-driven women to train like athletes." Every DM automation message mentions it. This is the moat — use it as the primary attack surface.
P2 · Meaningful Gap
No Named Mechanism — Commoditized Offer Architecture
Current offer: "coaching program at $1,200." No named protocol, no specific timeline with milestones, no unique mechanism that a competitor cannot copy. The result is that prospects are comparing Cindy to every other coach at similar or lower price points. A 3× Olympian with no named mechanism is competing against a 24-year-old Instagram influencer on price. This is structurally wrong.
The Fix (Jordan + JAN recommendation): Run the Offer Genie (JAN) to rebuild. Working mechanism name: "The Olympian Protocol" or "The Athlete Recomposition Method." Structure: 12-week minimum with defined milestones (Week 4: performance baseline established; Week 8: composition shift measurable; Week 12: athlete identity reactivated). Premium positioning: "The same principles I used at the Olympics, rebuilt for your life at 35." Named mechanism = higher price = better clients = fewer calls needed.
OK · Validated Asset — Protect This
Faith Integration + Athletic Authority Combination
The specific combination of elite athletic credibility AND faith-based coaching is virtually uncontested in the online fitness space. Most faith coaches don't have Olympic medals. Most Olympian coaches don't lead with faith. This is an extremely defensible market position that creates deep loyalty, strong referral culture, and insulation from commodity competition. Do not abandon or dilute either pillar to chase a broader audience.
Maintain: Keep both pillars prominent in all brand positioning. The faith angle is not a weakness for the right buyer — it is a pre-qualifying filter that increases commitment, retention, and lifetime value. Clients who share Cindy's faith values stay longer, refer more, and are more invested in the program outcomes.
