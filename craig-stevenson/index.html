Section 04
Constraint Diagnosis
P1 — Critical / Growth Blocker
3,000 Client Stories Are Completely Invisible Online
The most credible coaching credential in this cohort — 3,000 lifetime transformed clients — does not appear anywhere in the discoverable digital presence. No case studies. No transformation stories. No “3,000 clients” stat in the bio, the content, or the ads. A prospective online coaching client searching for a coach has no way to discover this proof. Craig’s offline reputation is earned. His online reputation does not yet exist — and it should be built entirely on the volume and specificity of this proof.
Fix: Every piece of content for the next 90 days opens with a 3,000-client reference or leads with a specific client case study. The bio must state the number. The first line of every hook must establish scale: “I’ve coached 3,000 people.” Start by contacting 10 past clients this week and asking for a 2-minute testimonial video or a written transformation story.
P1 — Critical / Immediate Opportunity
Past Client List Has Never Been Reactivated
Thousands of past clients who already know Craig, already trust him, and have already experienced his results have never received a direct outreach asking them to re-engage or refer others. This is the highest-ROI action Craig can take — a 9-word email to a warm list converts at dramatically higher rates than cold ads, costs nothing, and can be executed in 48 hours.
Fix: This week. Compile every email and phone number from past clients you can access. Send: “Hey [Name] — are you still interested in getting back in shape?” That’s it. Reply to the yes answers with: “I’m launching an online coaching program — would you want to hop on a quick call to see if it’s a fit?” Target: 7–10 clients contracted before any ad dollars are spent.
P2 — Meaningful Gap
Fragmented Digital Brand — Hard to Find Online
The digital presence is described as fragmented and difficult to find. For a coach with 3,000 clients and 21K potential followers on @thetotalcoach, this is a significant gap. Anyone who hears about Craig through word of mouth and tries to find him online hits friction — which means referrals that should convert don’t. The brand needs a unified home: one handle, one URL, one offer landing page.
Fix: Consolidate to @thetotalcoach as primary handle. Build one landing page: The Total Transformation System, 3,000-client proof stat front and center, 3 case study clips, one CTA (book a free strategy call). Link this page from every profile and every piece of content within 2 weeks.
P3 — Watch Item
Offer Price Point Needs Anchoring Before Online Launch
The goal is “$500+/month” for online clients. This is reasonable but needs a defined program structure: what does the client get, for how long, at what price? Without a specific number and specific deliverable, the sales conversation becomes vague and close rates drop. Anchor this before outreach begins.
Fix: Define The Total Transformation System as a 6-month program at $500/month ($3,000 contracted). Deliverables: custom training program, weekly check-in calls, nutrition framework, accountability messaging. Write it down before the first outreach call. Simple and specific beats elaborate and vague.
OK — Validated Asset
Offline Business Funds the Online Build — No Desperation
Craig’s 100 active in-person clients provide consistent cash flow during the online launch phase. This means he is not running ads from a position of desperation, not cutting prices to get clients, and not making panic decisions based on short-term revenue needs. This is a significant strategic advantage over pre-revenue prospects who need their online business to work immediately.
Maintain: Use the offline business to fund 60–90 days of patient online building. Do not cut in-person clients too quickly. Build the online model to 15+ clients before reducing the in-person load significantly.
