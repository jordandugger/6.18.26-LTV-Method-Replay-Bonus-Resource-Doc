Section 04
Constraint Diagnosis
P1 — Critical
No Named Niche Within Physical Limitations
Physical limitations is a category, not a niche. Back pain, post-surgery recovery, and MS are all very different audiences requiring different content, different hooks, and different offers. Without picking one, content is too broad to resonate with anyone.
Fix: Choose one primary limitation to lead with (recommendation: chronic back pain or post-injury recovery — largest addressable markets). Build all content, hooks, and the offer around that limitation first. Expand later.
P1 — Critical
No Digital Presence — Undiscoverable to People Who Need Him
Someone searching “fitness coach for back pain” or “coach who works with physical limitations” cannot find Drew. Zero social content means zero inbound. The audience exists and is actively searching — but there is no content to find.
Fix: Start publishing 3 reels/week using the hook bank above. Create a Google Business profile if local. Set up one clear social handle with the niche in the bio.
P2 — Gap
Offer Needs Structure and Price
No defined program structure, duration, or price. Without these, even warm referrals that result in a DM conversation cannot close efficiently.
Fix: Define the program: 12 weeks, weekly coaching calls + programming + messaging support, $800–$1,200. Write it down before the next client conversation.
OK — Validated
Clients Are Getting Results and Referring
Word-of-mouth in a sensitive niche means trust is being earned and results are real. This is the foundation of a scalable testimonial and content machine.
Fix: Start collecting testimonials immediately. Every current client is asked for a written or video result statement this month.
