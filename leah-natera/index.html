Section 04
Constraint Diagnosis
P1 — Critical
$300/Month Pricing Makes $20K/Month Mathematically Impossible
At $300/month, hitting $20K/month requires 67 concurrent active clients. That is a full-time operations and delivery problem, not a business. At $1,500 per 3-month program, the same goal requires 13 new clients. The pricing is the single biggest structural constraint in the business.
Fix: Reprice to a 3-month Gym Confidence + Macro Mastery Program at $1,500. This week. Message the 8 past clients offering them the new program structure. Run new clients at the new price only.
P2 — Gap
FB Group Leads Not Converting — Buyer Quality and Offer Clarity Issues
Facebook group leads at this price point often attract non-buyers or window shoppers. The conversion problem may be the price mismatch (low-price positioning attracts price shoppers) or an unclear offer presentation in the group.
Fix: Stop leading with price in FB group. Lead with the transformation: “I help women feel completely at home in the gym in 90 days.” When leads DM, ask qualifying questions before sharing the price. Book a call. Present the $1,500 program. The higher price filters out non-buyers.
P3 — Watch
No Instagram/Short-Form Content Presence
FB group is the only acquisition channel. No Instagram, no TikTok, no reels means no organic reach beyond the group. Long-term, the business needs at least one short-form content channel to grow beyond the group ceiling.
Fix: Start with one reel per week on Instagram. Use the gym confidence angle — it has strong visual potential (showing the gym environment, demonstrating exercises, teaching technique).
OK — Validated
The Offer Concept Converts — 8 Past Clients Prove It
8 people have paid. The concept works. The only thing that needs to change is the packaging and price.
Fix: Document what the successful past client conversations looked like. What did they ask? What convinced them? Build that insight into the new sales script.
