P1 · Primary Constraint — Killing Growth
Monthly Subscription Model with Low-Ticket Tier — Broken Economics
The $197/month tier is not just a pricing error — it is a business model error. At $197/month, Carlos needs 101 clients to hit $20K. Even the blended average at $600/month requires 34 concurrent clients from zero audience and zero marketing. Monthly subscriptions without commitment have near-zero retention leverage; the client can cancel after one month with no consequence. This creates perpetual churn, constant re-selling, and impossible math for hitting any meaningful revenue target.
The Fix (Jordan's exact prescription): Eliminate the $197 tier entirely. Move to outcome-based 6-month contract at $700/month ($4,200 total). Position as "Match IQ Transformation Program" — 6 months because measurable tactical improvement requires sustained practice and pattern repetition. Frame on sales calls as: "My program is a 6-month commitment because Match IQ is not a skill you develop in 30 days — it is a complete rewiring of how you see the court. Players who commit for the full arc see 80%+ match performance improvement." Add a 12-month backend at $400/month for players who reach 4.0+ and want to continue optimizing.
P1 · Primary Constraint — Trust and Distribution Gap
Zero Personal Brand — Carlos Is Invisible in His Own Business
16 Instagram followers, zero face-to-camera content, and a brand page that reads as a content publisher rather than a coach. In high-ticket online coaching, people buy the coach — not the brand. Carlos's in-person close rate is 70%+ because prospects are seeing his expertise, energy, and conviction firsthand. Online, that trust has to be built through video presence before any sale can happen. The content quality is there. The person behind it is not.

P2 · Meaningful Gap
Brand-New Social Account — Zero Trust Infrastructure Online
16 followers and 17 posts represents near-zero social proof. A prospect who finds Carlos's profile after seeing a paid ad will see a page that looks new and unestablished — which immediately creates friction in the purchase decision, especially for a high-ticket online program. The warm network launch mitigates this because past players already know Carlos offline, but any cold traffic funnel faces an immediate trust deficit before there is enough organic content to signal credibility.
The Fix: Run warm network outreach before launching any paid ads. Every warm launch client becomes a testimonial, a transformation screenshot, and a content asset. By day 45, the profile should have 30+ posts with Carlos on camera, some engagement, and early client proof — enough to run paid ads without the cold-start friction.
P2 · Meaningful Gap
No Backend Offer — Missing $4,800 Per Client in LTV
The 6-month front-end contract at $700/month is correct. But Carlos's existing in-person data tells a critical story: his clients stay 12–18 months. That retention behavior does not disappear online — it just needs to be captured in a structured backend. Without a 12-month continuity program, clients who complete the 6-month program either stop (lost revenue and lost transformation) or renew at the same rate ad-hoc (leaving money and commitment on the table). The LTV ceiling is artificially low.
The Fix: Build the 12-month backend offer (Jordan's recommendation: $400/month) as a "Match IQ Mastery Continuation" program. Position during the initial sales call: "The 6-month program gets you to 4.0 level tactical IQ. The continuation program is where 4.0 players start becoming 4.5+." Pre-frame the 18-month journey from day one of the sales call so it never feels like an upsell — it is the complete transformation arc. Full LTV at $700 × 6 + $400 × 12 = $9,000 per client.
